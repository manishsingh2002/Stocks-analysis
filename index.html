Chapter 01

THE FUNDAMENTAL
TRUTH OF CHARTS

Before learning any indicator, you must understand price action. A chart is the collective footprint of millions of buy and sell decisions. Every candle tells a story of greed, fear, conviction, and panic.

CANDLESTICK ANATOMYFOUNDATIONAL
01 — Direction & Size

Who Won the Session?

Green body = buyers won. Red body = sellers won. The SIZE of the body matters. A massive body indicates conviction and institutional participation. Tiny bodies indicate indecision.

02 — The Wicks (Shadows)

How Hard Was the Fight?

Long wicks represent intraday drama. Price moved far but was violently rejected. High-wick candles near support or resistance are the most powerful reversal signals in trading.

Chapter 02

THE 3-TIMEFRAME
METHOD

Professional analysts zoom in like a sniper. The higher timeframe is the absolute boss. Never take a daily trade that contradicts the weekly trend.

Monthly (Macro)

The Big Picture

Are we in a structural bull or bear market? Nifty's monthly chart has shown a clear uptrend since 2003. This is your compass. Never short a monthly uptrend blindly.

Weekly (Trend)

The Direction

Your bible. Above the 21-week EMA = you should be long. Below it = caution. The weekly chart separates temporary retail noise from real institutional trend changes.

Daily (Entry)

The Precision

Where you pull the trigger. Look for pullbacks to the 20 EMA, support zones, and reversal candles. You decided direction on the weekly—now find the precise price.

The Non-Negotiable Rule A daily buy signal in a weekly downtrend is a trap. A weekly buy signal in a monthly uptrend is an opportunity. Align the stars before risking capital.
Chapter 03

MARKET STRUCTURE

Market structure is the framework. Everything else is derived from it. Read the sequence of swing highs and lows to map the institutional footprint.

MARKET CYCLEUNIVERSAL LAW
UPTREND (Bullish)

Higher Highs (HH) + Higher Lows (HL)

Each rally makes a new high. Each pullback stops above the previous low. This proves buyers are willing to step in at higher prices. The trend is intact until a Lower Low is printed.

DOWNTREND (Bearish)

Lower Highs (LH) + Lower Lows (LL)

Each rally fails below the previous peak. Each decline breaks the previous floor. Sellers dominate. Do not try to "catch the falling knife" here.

Chapter 04

INDICATOR DEEP DIVE

Indicators don't predict the future; they describe current momentum, volatility, and trend. Use them to confirm price action, not replace it.

The Moving Averages

EMA System (20, 50, 200)

Exponential Moving Averages weight recent price data heavier.
20 EMA: Short-term trend pulse. Pullbacks here are entries.
50 EMA: Medium-term institutional support.
200 EMA: The ultimate macro line. Above = Bull, Below = Bear.

Momentum Oscillator

MACD (12, 26, 9)

Shows the relationship between two EMAs. The Histogram crossing the zero line is a powerful momentum shift. A bullish crossover deep below the zero line often signals a major bottom in indices.

Chapter 05

ADVANCED ORDER FLOW

This separates retail from pros. Understanding where algorithmic liquidity resides and where heavy volume was historically transacted.

Fibonacci Retracement

The Algorithmic Golden Pocket

Markets don't move in straight lines. They retrace. Institutional algorithms heavily buy pullbacks hitting the 50% to 61.8% retracement levels of a major swing. Combine this with prior resistance-turned-support for A+ setups.

Volume Profile (VPVR)

Price vs. Volume Gravity

Standard volume shows time. VPVR shows volume at specific *price levels*. The Point of Control (POC) is the price with the most historical volume. It acts as a massive magnet and support/resistance level.

Chapter 06

CHART PATTERNS
DECODED

Patterns are visual representations of human psychology—greed, fear, and capitulation repeating over time. Learn the story, not just the shape.

BullishContinuation

Cup & Handle

A rounded bottom showing institutional accumulation, followed by a tight "handle" (shakeout). Breakout above the rim yields massive moves.

BearishReversal

Head & Shoulders

The classic top. Three peaks, the middle is highest. Shows buyers exhausting. A break below the "neckline" confirms the trend reversal.

BullishContinuation

Bull Flag

A violent, high-volume "pole" followed by a low-volume, downward drifting channel. It’s the market digesting gains before the next leg up.

Chapter 07

CANDLESTICK SIGNALS

Context is everything. A hammer candle in the middle of a range means nothing. A hammer at the 200 EMA is a high-probability trigger.

The Hammer (Pin Bar)

Long lower wick (2x body). Sellers pushed hard, buyers violently rejected them. Highest win-rate when found at major support.

Bullish Engulfing

The green candle's body completely swallows the prior red body. A massive shift in psychology and a sudden surge in demand.

Shooting Star

The inverse hammer. Bulls tried to breakout, but institutional sellers dumped supply, leaving a long upper wick. Sell signal at resistance.

Chapter 08

INDIA-SPECIFIC TOOLS

Indian markets (NSE/BSE) offer unique data points. Professional analysts fuse these metrics with technicals to gauge true market sentiment.

Sentiment Gauge

India VIX (Volatility Index)

Measures expected volatility based on Nifty options.
• < 12: Complacency. High risk of sudden correction.
• 13 - 18: Normal healthy trending environment.
• > 25: Extreme fear. Historically a zone to buy quality stocks.

Contrarian Indicator

PCR (Put-Call Ratio)

Total Puts / Total Calls. If PCR > 1.4, the market is excessively bearish (oversold) and primed for a short-covering rally. If PCR < 0.6, extreme euphoria—caution advised.

Institutional Flow

FII / DII Data & Open Interest

Foreign Institutional Investors (FII) drive major trends, while Domestic Institutional Investors (DII) provide structural support (SIP flows). In F&O, rising Price + rising Open Interest (OI) = Long Buildup (Bullish). Falling Price + rising OI = Short Buildup (Bearish).

Chapter 09

RISK & POSITION SIZING

The holy grail of trading isn't an indicator; it's math. If you do not calculate position size based on a defined stop loss, you are gambling.

The 1% Rule Formula

Fixed Fractional Sizing

Never risk more than 1-2% of your total capital on a single trade. The size of your position adapts to the width of your stop loss.

Account: ₹10,00,000
Max Risk (1%): ₹10,000

Entry: ₹500 | Stop: ₹480
Risk Per Share: ₹20

Size: 10,000 / 20 = 500 Shares
Asymmetric R:R (Risk to Reward) Only take trades offering at least 1:2 or 1:3 R:R. If you risk ₹10,000, your target must yield ₹20,000+. With 1:2 R:R, you can be wrong 50% of the time and still be highly profitable.
The Drawdown Trap Lose 10% → Need 11% to recover.
Lose 50% → Need 100% to recover.
This is why cutting losses ruthlessly at the structural invalidation point is mandatory. Hope is not a strategy.

STEP 1: MACRO ALIGNMENT

The Weekly Tail-Wind

The index (e.g., Nifty) is in a confirmed weekly uptrend, printing Higher Highs. The specific stock is outperforming the index (Relative Strength) and is comfortably above its 21-week EMA. You have the wind at your back.

STEP 2: LIQUIDITY SWEEP

The Daily POC Retest

Price pulls back into a high-volume node (Point of Control) that aligns perfectly with the 50-61.8% Fibonacci Golden Pocket. Price briefly dips *below* support to trigger retail stop losses (Liquidity Sweep), then violently rejects back up.

STEP 3: EXECUTION TRIGGER

The Hourly Bull Flag / Hammer

Zoom down to the 1-hour chart. The violent rejection forms a massive Hammer candle with high volume. You enter your long position exactly when the price breaks above the high of that Hammer candle. Precision entry.

Step 4: The Math (Management) Stop loss is placed 1 ATR (Average True Range) below the Hammer wick. Position size is restricted to 1% of total portfolio value. Target 1 is at the previous swing high (locking in 1:2 R:R). Target 2 is left as a "runner," trailing behind the 20-day EMA to catch a massive trend. This is how professionals trade.
